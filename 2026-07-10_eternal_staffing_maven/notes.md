## Key Concepts

### Brook's Law

Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later. Coordination overhead and communication paths grow quadratically with team size, dominating productivity gains from additional hands.

### U-Curves

Trade-off analysis tool showing typical min-max patterns:

- **Y-axis:** Cost, cycle time, or quality degradation
- **X-axis:** Team size or staffing allocation
- **U-shape:** Reveals an optimal point where adding more resources switches from beneficial to harmful; typically curves at team sizes 3-7 (single team) and 20-100 (organization-level).

### Conway's Law

Organization's communication structure shapes system architecture. Software design mirrors the structure of the organization that produced it; misalignment between org chart and desired system design generates friction.

### Sheward Charts (Control Charts for Processes)

Statistical visualization tracking process output over time with upper/lower control limits (typically ±3σ from mean). Signals when variation is common cause (random, within limits) vs. special cause (assignable, out of limits). Foundation for process stability assessment before seeking improvement.

### Process Control Charts

Broader class of statistical charts monitoring process behavior. Includes Sheward charts, trend analysis, run charts. Used in quality assurance to detect drift, instability, or anomalies. Establishes baseline "in-control" state before declaring improvement or regression.

## Signals & Levers Book

Hendrickson and Tosi's forthcoming book (Fall 2026, IT Revolution) frames software delivery obstacles through systems thinking. Core idea: identify signals (problem indicators) and apply levers (intervention mechanisms) at system level rather than attacking symptoms in isolation. Practical frameworks for unblocking delivery by treating the system holistically.
